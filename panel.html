Panel Discussion


Communication of cultural differences in HCI design process

More and more products are aimed at international target audiences. Working in international environments and with a global audience provides a number of challenges that has to do with the understanding of cultural differences and the communication of these.

A recent research (July 2014) on how large companies perform international user studies found that companies do not collect data about end users in all the countries/regions they operate in. Instead, they focus on a few strategic markets. The researchers prefer to do field studies and to go to the field themselves to gain rich insights and to control the data collection process. Furthermore the companies are in the process of finding out how to best present the insights about international end users to their employees. So far, a best practice for incorporating both national cultural differences and cross-cultural similarities into persona descriptions, segmentations, etc. has not been found.

This points to several challenges and the panel will discuss:

  • What strategies should we apply when we collect data in order to get data from the proper geographic areas?
  • How do we communicate the cultural differences?
  • When working with personas or other types of user characteristics how do we get the cultural differences described?


Panelists

 


Ms. Apala Lahiri Chavan

Apala is a world-renowned expert on cross cultural design and contextual innovation. Her innovative and pioneering techniques have benefitted global giants such as HP Labs, Adidas, Nokia, Sony Ericsson, NCR, and Intel, among others. She is also recipient of the International Audi Design Award.

She is also a dynamic and creative instructor and author. Her keynote talks on contextual innovation, ecosystem research, internationalization, and designing for emerging markets have received acclaim in USA, Canada, Europe, India, and China. She has developed a vast array of data-gathering techniques such as Bollywood Method, Bizarre Bazaar, and Funky Facilitator, which help understand the user experience in diverse cultural and economic environments. She and her team pioneered the “Ecosystem Chart” that organizes vast amounts of ethnographic data into a coherent model.

Apala also specializes in creating UX strategy for organizations who are looking at creating breakthrough user experience for their customers and other stakeholders. Some of her recent projects include:

  1. UX strategy for cross channel integration of one of the largest banks in South Africa.
  2. Ecosystem research and innovation strategy for a large German appliance maker.
  3. Research, innovation and design for the in home entertainment domain in India, China and USA.
  4. Ecosystem research about cell phones for the Indian market for a large Finnish mobile phone maker.
  5. Microcredit system, managing small loans to farmers and small businesses, with operators who could only read numbers, not text for MIT Media Lab Asia.
  6. Researching media applications in rural markets for an American technology company.
  7. Applying handwriting recognition in small businesses in India.
  8. Unusual applications for ATM machines.
  9. Identifying latent needs in emerging markets for a US based home medical equipment company, used in the therapy process.
  10. Innovation and strategy for Inclusive banking in Kenya.
  11. Innovation and strategy for mobile banking in South Africa.

Apala has been with HFI since 1999 as Managing Director, HFI India, and VP, HFI Asia, Chief Oracle and Innovator before assuming her current role. In addition to her usability certifications (CUA and CXA), Apala holds an MSc with distinction in User Interface Design from London Guildhall University. She is also a TEDx speaker. Her TEDx Talk is titled 'Three Laws of UX'

 


Dr. Erik Grönvall

Dr. Erik Grönvall is Associate Professor at the IT University of Copenhagen (ITU), Denmark. At ITU, he works in the ‘People and Computational Things’-section, the Interaction Design Group. Erik Grönvall’s research interest lies in the role technology has in everyday life, the notion of participation, how people appropriate technology, and how technology can be designed to better align with the capabilities and needs of diverse users.

Cultural and (inter-)national aspects of technology design and use is part of Erik Grönvall’s current research interests – it spans from designing and building specific healthcare technologies to investigate the use of such technologies and the societal effects. Furthermore Erik Grönvall designs and constructs physical computing (e.g. shape-changing) objects and researches how people appropriate and understand such systems. Erik has been the manager of a number of different projects that   included academia and industry.



Dr. Lene Nielsen

Dr. Lene Nielsen is Denmark’s leading specialist in personas and the first in the world to write a PhD on personas.

Lene Nielsen is the manager of Center for Personas Research and application. The Center helps industry to understand their end-users and has performed a number of research projects.

Lene Nielsen is Associate Professor in the research group People & Computational Things and teaches in the areas of concept development, target group analysis, and interaction design.

She has many years of experience as a consultant in helping companies use personas – both within the private and the public sector. She has published numerous articles on personas and how to use the method in the design process and two books: “Personas – User Focused Design” and the Danish book  “Personas – brugerfokuseret design”.



Dr. Narasimha Kamath

Dr. Narasimha Kamath is the product director at JDA Software. He earned his fellowship (PhD) in SCM from IIM Calcutta and was honoured with the outstanding doctoral research award from European Foundation.

Dr. Narasimha Kamath has worked extensively with customers across all industry verticals, different regions and is recognized for his thought leadership. He has many international publications to his credit, including US patents.

He leads the new product introduction and innovation activities at the Centre of Excellence in India.   He also fosters the industry - academic collaboration creating a win-win environment.



Prof. Pradeep Yammiyavar FDRS

Prof. Pradeep Yammiyavar is a senior professor working in the area of multi and interdisciplinary programs related to Innovation, Design , Management & Technology with 30 years experience in multiple roles of Scientist, Researcher, Teacher and Designer of Products and product systems with emphasis on User Centered Design, Usability Engineering.

Prof. Pradeep Yammiyavar has Professional Qualifications in multiple disciplines - Engineering, Design, Psychology & Urban Infrastructure planning and he is a consultant to IT & Manufacturing Industries. He is advisor to Creative Disciplines in the Industrial & Educational Sector. He is Scientific adviser to government in areas such as IPR, Innovation & Knowledge Incubation. He is currently Professor and Head of the Centre for Educational Technology. Also Professor of Design Research in the Department of Design. He is Mentor & Guide for 100s of successful Design professionals well placed worldwide. He has active International collaborations with institutions in Europe through research projects.

Prof. Prdeep Yammiyavar is author of over 100 research publications and 9 IPRs. He has been honored with awards & citations both nationally and internationally.

He has been strategic adviser to design activities, curriculum restructuring & up-gradation. Established new bench marks & standards in Design Research & Education in India, Established new thrust areas in Design such as HCI, Interaction design, Information design. He had leadership & pioneering role in establishing Design Education centers at IISc Bangalore (M.Des Design Program - CPDM ) & Interaction Design Program at IITG. He has set up India's first Usability Engineering Research Laboratory that nurtures PhDs in all areas of Design including Cultural studies Product Design and HCI.
