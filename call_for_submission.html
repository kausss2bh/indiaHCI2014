Call for Submission

India HCI 2014 invites original and unpublished contributions for four tracks - full and short papers, posters and demos, professional case studies and student design consortium. Full papers (8-10 pages) and short papers (4-6 pages) would articulate thorough and systematic research in any thematic area related to HCI. Professional case studies (3-5 pages) would document the design process and outcomes of an interactive product in a professional context. Posters and Demos (4'*3' size poster and/or working application) would showcase late-breaking research, design, or technology. And the student design consortium (4-6 pages) provides a platform for students of any discipline to share their interaction design work with a wider audience.

Full and Short Papers

Original research work may be submitted either as full papers (8-10 pages) or short papers (4-6 pages). All contributions should be of high quality, original and not published elsewhere or submitted for publication during the review period. Submissions must be blinded for peer review. Every submission must identify the theme which best relates to the contents of the paper. Papers will be peer reviewed by the Program Committee and may be accepted for presentation.

Student Design Consortium

For the last two years, India HCI is organising Student Design Consortium. If you are a full-time student (or have been a full-time student until recently) and have designed an interactive product, either as an academic activity or as a hobby, you can submit the same to SDC. After a review, selected entries will be presented in a session during the conference, where you or your team will get opportunity to showcase your work. This could be an opportunity for you to win accolades, explore possibility of implementing your ideas and get valuable feedback from experts.

Important Information
Submission Format Anonymised submission consisting of Extended Abstract(max 6 pages) in the ACM case studies format, list of references and a 5 minutes video.
Submission Size Maximum 20 MB (abstract + video)
Submission Deadline 19 Aug 26 Aug
Other Dates
Acceptance Notification Date 10 September
Camera Ready Submission Date 01 October
Eligibility

You are eligible to submit an entry to SDC if you currently are an undergraduate or masters level student in any discipline (including design, engineering, architecture, arts, commerce, management etc.), or if you finished your education in the calendar year 2014. A submission could be authored by 1-5 people. They need not be from the same educational institute, but all of them must be eligible to submit to the SDC. Faculty members or professionals who may have contributed as guides or supervisors would be acknowledged separately and should not be listed as "authors" of the submission.

Submission

Your submission will consist of a extended abstract (max 6 pages), list of references and a 5-minute video file. Your submission size can be maximum 20MB (abstract + video). Both the abstract and the video must be previously unpublished (other than within an educational institute) and the authors should own copyright of all material that they submit. The abstract and the video should detail out the project outcome and methodology in brief and interesting way. For the process of the review, the abstract and the video submission should be anonymised (i.e. all references to the authors, their institutes or their locations must be removed.

Submission Process

Submission for this track must be made through the easychair conference management system. For detailed instruction please refer: Submission Guidelines

NOTE: The submissions for the review in the SDC track must be completely blinded - no reference should be made of the people involved in the project including names of author(s)', faculty member(s), practitioner(s), institution(s) affiliated with etc. In the initial entry, authors are requested to use discretion in using names or pictures of locations (locations such as Powai or Paldi are dead giveaways). Authors may refer to their own work as part of the references, but similar care must be taken. If any of the prohibited information is necessary, the version submitted to the review should temporarily be censor out (Xxxx in text, and a beep in the audio track would be sufficient). You will have an opportunity to edit upon the submission once it is accepted.

Competition Structure

Stage 1: The primary reviews for the submissions in the SDC track will be done by peer students (including those who graduated in calendar year 2014). The mentors associated with this track will only allot reviewers and write the meta-reviews. This will ensure that the only the most original work gets selected.

Stage 2: The authors will be given an opportunity to showcase their work in the foyer. The videos will be screened followed by a short question and answer session with the authors. A team of evaluators will select top 10 entries which will be given opportunity to present their work with the audience.

Stage 3: Top 10 entries will be give opportunity to give 5 minute presentation to the audience. In this round, top 3 entries will be selected and who will receive the IndiaHCI 2014 Student Designer of the Year Award.

For any query contact studentchair@indiahci2014.in




Posters and Demos

The Indian conference in HCI invites submissions from students, professionals, researchers and academicians in the demo and poster category. The topics for demo and posters should address areas presented in conference topics, but other areas of HCI are also encouraged. We encourage HCI practitioners to submit their ongoing and emerging work on innovative and novel ideas, interactive demos involving novel interaction techniques. Research explorations and demonstrations of novel technological interventions from students and startups are highly encouraged for submission in this category. Submissions in this category will be peer reviewed by the program committee based on its novelty and originality of concepts, feasibility and concreteness. Anonymity of authors and reviewers will be retained during the review process for submissions in this category.

Submission Instructions for Posters

All submissions must be submitted in PDF file in extended abstract SIGCHI format. The submission must be between 2-4 pages. All submissions are encouraged to submit A4 size poster for getting inputs on the visual design of their posters. Authors of accepted submissions will be intimated to submit A1 size posters prior to conference dates.

Professional Design Case Studies

Designers working in industry often find themselves doing great design. It makes a difference to users by delivering great product experience and the stakeholders, including the designers, are happy with the outcome. If you have experienced this in your practice, you may want to write a professional case study and share it with others in the industry so that your work gets further recognition. You can send such a case study to India HCI 2014 conference and reap huge benefits.

Why Write a Professional Case Study and submit it to India HCI 2014?

Professional Case Studies that you will submit to this conference will be reviewed by a panel of peers who are also domain experts. This is a double blinded review process, which means that a reviewer has no idea who or which organization has written the case study. The conference chairpersons take due precautions to ensure fairness and lack of bias in the review process. At the end of the review process, you will get either an acceptance or a rejection, and a feedback form the reviewers.

Reviewers are recognized HCI industry experts, academicians and researchers. Thus, each selected case study is carefully reviewed and chosen as a contribution of significance to the profession. Case studies chosen by the reviewers as the best ones may be given slots for presentation during the conference case studies track. Chosen case study authors will be able to present, discuss and interact with conference participants, some of which could also be future clients for your business or other interested parties.

The Process of Submitting a Professional Case Study

A professional case study should include introduction, background of the project, method, design deliverables and demos. It could present the conclusions and discussion topics. A submission may be authored by 1-5 people. They need not be from the same organization and not necessarily part of HCI group in your organization.

The submissions must be completely blinded - no reference should be made to the author(s)' names or practitioner(s) involved with the project, or names of the organization(s) the author(s) are affiliated with. In the initial entry, authors are requested to use discretion in using names or pictures of locations. Authors may refer to their own work as part of the references, but similar care must be taken. If any of the prohibited information is necessary, the version submitted to the review should temporarily censor it out (Xxxx in text, and a beep in the audio track would be sufficient). You will have an opportunity to edit the submission once it is accepted.

The primary reviews for the submissions in the industry case study track will be done by peers – industry professionals and academicians. This will ensure that the best case studies are selected among the submissions.

The best submissions will be presented during the conference. The videos, if any, will be screened followed by a short question and answer session with the authors.

Format
  • Submissions will consist of a 150-word abstract, a list of references and a short case study paper along with any media/demonstration video.
  • The abstract or the short paper must be previously unpublished and the authors should own copyright of all material that they submit. The short paper must adhere to the ACM guidelines.
  • The final submission is expected to be a 5/6 page document either in the Extended Abstract format or the ACM paper format.
  • Templates: Extended abstract SIGCHI format, ACM case studies format

Submission Guidelines

India HCI follows a double blind review process for all the submissions at the conference. This means that during and after the review process, the reviewers do not know who the author(s) were and the author(s) do not know who reviewed their papers.At the time of submission, authors are expected to ensure that their submissions have been blinded.

Authors should, however, keep citations to all previous work including their own, so that the reviewers can ensure that all previous research has been taken into account. Authors should cite their own work in the third person, for example, “As described by Dalvi et. al, …”. Further suppression of your identity in the body of the paper (e.g. name of town /country, participating institutions, photographs of researchers etc.) is left to author’s discretion.

All authors will be notified of acceptance or rejection latest by the dates mentioned against each category along with the review comments on their submissions. Based on the individual comments, authors will be instructed to submit the Camera Ready version of their submissions on or before the advertised dates against different categories. The accepted submissions must be presented at the conference by one of the author(s). The presented works will be published in the conference proceedings.
